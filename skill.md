## 2. Core Skill — Knowledge Experience Design

**What it is:**
The practice of shaping how knowledge is encountered — not just what is said, but in what order, at what pace, with what visual logic, and through what kind of interaction.

This is not slide design. It is not content writing. It is not web development.
It is the synthesis of all three, guided by learning architecture and experience strategy.

**The underlying belief:**
Information doesn't change people. Experience does.
A well-designed knowledge page or workshop doesn't just inform — it creates a shift in how the audience thinks.

## 3. What I Do (Public Scope)

### 3a. Workshop & Presentation Design
- Turn a speaker's ideas into a structured, sequentially-revealed slide experience
- Design "knowledge flows" — the order in which concepts are introduced matters
- Build interactive HTML presentations (not PowerPoint) with animation, reveal logic, and visual hierarchy
- Applied at: university workshops, corporate training, keynote talks, online courses

### 3b. Knowledge Page Design
- Single-page sites that function as a methodology showcase, course landing page, or thought leadership hub
- Not a portfolio. Not a brochure. A *demonstration* of thinking.
- Structured like a workshop: premise → framework → evidence → application → CTA
- The page itself is proof of the skill

### 3c. Personal Course & Learning Architecture
- Help educators, coaches, and knowledge workers structure their expertise into teachable, shareable frameworks
- Identify the "minimum viable framework" — the core mental model behind someone's work
- Design the curriculum arc: what comes first, what is revealed when, what triggers insight

### 3d. Interactive Content Systems
- Sequential reveal logic (ideas that unfold as the audience is ready)
- Accordion structures for layered depth
- Visual frameworks (DFV, MECE, value formulas, Octalysis) rendered as interactive components
- Designed for both live presentation and async self-paced reading
